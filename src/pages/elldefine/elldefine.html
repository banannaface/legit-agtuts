Defining Ellipse

 An ellipse is the set of all points on a plane whose distance from two fixed points F1 and F2 add up to a constant. Both points are focus, and together they are called foci.


 Given are two points on the x-axis, F1(-c, 0) and F2(c, 0), the foci, both c units away from their center (0, 0). See Figure above. Let P(x, y) be a point on the ellipse. Let the common sum of the distances be 2a. Thus, we have PF1 + PF2 = 2a
