Defining Hyperbola

 An hyperbola looks sort of like two mirrored parabolas, with the two "halves" being called "branches". Like an ellipse, an hyperbola has two foci and two vertices; unlike an ellipse, the foci in an hyperbola are further from the hyperbola's center than are its vertices.

center: origin (0,0)
foci: F1(-c,0) and F2(c,0)

  • each focus is c units away from the center.
  • for any point in the ellipse, the sum of its distance from both focus is 2a.

vertices: V1(-a,0) and V2(a,0)

  • if y=0 then x = ±a. Each vertex is a units away from the center.
  • the segment V1V2 is the major axis with length 2a.

covertices: W1(0,-b) and W2(0,b)

  • if x=0 then y = ±b. Each covertex is b units away from the center.
  • the segment W1W2 is the minor axis with length 2b.
