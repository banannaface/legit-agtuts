Solving Circle

Problem 1

 A street with two lanes, each 10 ft wide, goes through a semicircular tunnel with radius 12 ft. How high is the tunnel at the edge of each lane?
Upon analyzing, the problem should look like this.

Figure 1
We draw a coordinate system with origin at the middle of the highway, as shown above.

Solution 1

In Figure 1, we can see that a right triangle was formed.
 Its hypothenuse as the radius of the circle which is 12.
 One leg of the triangle is 10ft as given by the problem.
 Find the other leg using the pythagorean theorem.

a2 + b2 = c2 pythagorean theorem 102 + b2 = 122 substitute given 100 + b2 = 144 square the given b2 = 144 - 100 transpose to the right b2 = 44 solve √b2 = √44 solve for the square root b = 6.63 The tunnel is 6.63ft high at the edge of each lane.

Solution 2

In Figure 1, we can see that the radius of the tunnel is 12.
 From the center to the edge of the lane is 10, which is the X.
 Point P is Y feet above 10.
 Find Y using the standard form equation.

x2 + y2 = r2 standard equation 102 + y2 = 122 substitute given 100 + y2 = 144 square the given y2 = 144 - 100 transpose to the right y2 = 44 solve √y2 = √44 solve for the square root y = 6.63 The tunnel is 6.63ft high at the edge of each lane.

Problem 2

 A piece of a broken plate was dug up in an archaeological site. It was put on top of a grid, as shown in Figure 2, with the arc of the plate passing through A(7, 0), B(1, 4) and C(7, 2). Find its center, and the standard equation of the circle describing the boundary of the plate.

Figure 2
The broken plate on top of a grid with its circumference passing through points A, B and C.

Solution 1

We fi rst have to determine the center. It is the intersection of the perpendicular bisectors of AB and BC, which are chords of the circle.

 First, we have to get the midpoint and slope of AB and BC.
 Use the point-slope form to get the equation of the perpendicular bisector of both points.
 Solve for the intersection of both perpendicular bisectors.
Solving for M (midpoint of AB) and N (midpoint of BC) Mx = (x1 + x2)/2 My = (y1 + y2)/2 midpoint formula Mx = (-7 + 1)/2 My = (0 + 4)/2 substitute given Mx = -3 My = 2 M = (-3, 2) Nx = (1 + 7)/2
Ny = (4 + 2)/2 substitute given Nx = 4 Ny = 3 N = (4, 3)
Solving for mAB (slope of AB) and mBC (slope of BC) mAB = (y2 - y1)/(x2 - x1) slope formula mAB = (4 - 0)/(1 + 7) substitute given mAB = 1/2 slope of line AB mBC = (2 - 4)/(7 - 1) substitute given mBC = -1/3 slope of line BC

 Since, M = (-3,2) and the negative reciprocal of mAB = -2, the equation of the first perpendicualr bisector is y = -2x - 4.

 While N = (4,3) and the negative reciprocal of mBC = 3, the equation of the second perpendicular bisector is y = 3x - 9.


Solving for the intersection of y = -2x - 4 and y = 3x - 9 -2x - 4 = 3x - 9 substitute the 1st to the 2nd equation 9 - 4 = 3x + 2x transpose like terms 5 = 5x solve x = 1 get value of x y = 3(1) - 9 substitute the value of x y = -6 center = (1,-6)

 Center is (1,-6), we can then get the radius by getting the distance between the center and any of points A, B, C. Radius would be 10 so the standard equation is
(x - 1)2 + (y + 6)2 = 100.
